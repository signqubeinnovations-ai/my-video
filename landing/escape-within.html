The Inner Journey

What happens when
you go quiet

Most people have never given themselves more than a moment of real silence. Here is what unfolds when you finally do.

1
Day One

The Arrival

The mind is still running. It will take a few hours to realise nobody is demanding anything of it. That is the first gift — permission to simply be.

2
Days Two & Three

The Deepening

Old thoughts surface and pass without holding you. The breath becomes something you can feel. Silence starts to seem less like absence — and more like presence.

3
Days Four & Five

The Opening

Meditation goes deeper than it ever has at home. Not because you are doing something different. Because everything else has stopped. This is what you came for.

4
After

The Return

You come back to the same world, the same people. But something has shifted at the root. The silence comes with you — and that changes everything.

Common Questions

Everything you need
to know

Do I need prior meditation experience?
Yes — these are advanced programs. You should have completed at least one Art of Living course (Happiness Program / Part 1 or equivalent). This ensures everyone in the group has a shared foundation and the retreat can go deep without needing to teach basics.
What does "silence retreat" actually mean?
During most of the retreat, participants maintain noble silence — no speaking, no phones, no reading, no writing. This isn't a restriction; it's a gift. When you stop talking, a deeper conversation with yourself becomes possible. Facilitators are available for essential communication.
What is the cost of the retreats?
We follow a contribution-based model with a recommended amount that covers accommodation, sattvic meals, and logistics. No one is turned away for financial reasons — if cost is a concern, please mention it during our call. All amounts are fully transparent.
Are these retreats affiliated with Art of Living Foundation?
We are a group of Art of Living volunteers who organise these programs independently with the spirit of seva. The programs draw from Art of Living teachings and practices. Please confirm current affiliation status with us directly if this is important for your decision.
What should I bring?
Comfortable, loose, natural-fibre clothing (white or light colours preferred), a personal yoga mat if you have one, any personal medications, and most importantly — an open heart. We'll send a complete checklist after you register. Leave your laptop at home.
Can I join if I have health conditions?
Please mention any physical or mental health conditions when you register. Our volunteer team will review and advise. Some conditions may require a doctor's clearance; we'll guide you through this process with care and confidentiality.
How do I travel to these locations?
All our locations are accessible by public or private transport. We share detailed travel guidance after confirmation, and often coordinate group transport from nearby railway stations or airports. Kerala's connectivity is excellent.

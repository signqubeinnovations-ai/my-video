A Virtual Reality experience unlike anything you've encountered — built around one of the most extraordinary lives of our time.

Gurudev Sri Sri Ravishankar

Founder of the Art of Living Foundation · Teacher to hundreds of millions · A man who walks the world asking us to breathe, smile, and look within. This exhibition brings his world to yours — in four immersive chapters.

01

Life Journey

Walk the Path of
an Extraordinary Life

A branching cinematic story spanning seven decades. Every choice you make reveals a new chapter — and a new part of yourself.

Sight

You stand in a quiet courtyard in Tamil Nadu, 1956. The light is the honey-gold of early morning. A young child sits cross-legged beneath a neem tree, eyes closed — already carrying something ancient.

Feel

The cold of the Himalayas wraps around you at 12,000 feet. Dawn touches the peaks first — a slow blush of pink across impossibly still snow — as a young seeker watches in silence, searching for the source of everything.

Witness

A circle of twenty people on a river bank, 1982. The first Sudarshan Kriya ever held. You are there for the moment a practice is born that will one day circle the globe.

Scale

Three million faces fill an open field. Gurudev speaks. Then — a silence deeper than you've ever felt — three million people exhale together. You are in the middle of it all.

"

When you know how to handle the breath, you can handle every situation in life. The breath is the link between the inner world and the outer world.

— Gurudev Sri Sri Ravishankar

02

Guided Meditation

Breathe Into
Absolute Stillness

In virtual reality the outside world falls completely away. Only the breath, Gurudev's voice, and the vast quiet within you.

Sight

The room around you dissolves. Stars emerge — not a ceiling of stars, but a universe of them surrounding you in every direction. You are floating in something enormous and perfectly calm.

Sound

Gurudev's voice arrives not from a speaker, not from a direction, but from everywhere at once. Gentle. Unhurried. "Take a long, deep breath in…" And something in you lets go.

Feel

With each breath, pulses of soft light move through the space around you — saffron on the inhale, deep violet on the exhale. Your body follows without being asked. The breath breathes you.

Arrive

The boundary between you and the surrounding stillness becomes unclear. You are not watching the stars anymore. You are somewhere beyond thought — resting in what Gurudev says is already there.

"

Meditation is not something you do. It is something that happens — when you stop doing everything else and simply be.

— Gurudev Sri Sri Ravishankar

03

Ashram Tour

A Sanctuary
Without Walls

The Art of Living International Centre in Bengaluru — one of the most peaceful places on Earth — now comes to you in every detail.

Sight

Morning mist moves slowly across a lotus pond. The water is entirely still. A white egret lifts off without a sound. Everywhere: layered greens — bamboo, banyan, bougainvillea — glowing in the first light.

Sound

Temple bells from somewhere unseen. A soft chant rising — swelling — then gently settling like water. You turn 360° and see only trees, paths, devotees in white moving with unhurried peace.

Enter

The doors of the Vishalakshi Mantap open before you. Light pours through the dome in long golden columns. Hundreds sit in silence. The air is dense with intention — you feel it land on your skin.

Stay

You sit on the steps at sunset. A peacock crosses the path. No one rushes. This is what the ashram gives everyone who comes — permission to stop. To breathe. To simply be where you are.

"

You are not just a body and a mind. You are a field of unbounded awareness — infinite peace, infinite joy, infinite love. This is your nature.

— Gurudev Sri Sri Ravishankar

04

Live Satsang

Feel the Joy of
Ten Thousand Souls

A satsang with Gurudev is felt more than described. Music, laughter, devotion, and a silence that holds them all. The front row comes to you.

Sound

Tablas, harmonium, voices — the bhajan begins and sound wraps around you from every direction. It builds. Your whole chest resonates. Ten thousand people are singing and you are among them — surrounded, held, moved.

Sight

A sea of candle flames stretches to the horizon. Faces lit gold from below — eyes closed, smiling, tears running freely. Joy and grief looking identical. You turn and see the same scene in every direction, endless and alive.

Presence

Gurudev speaks. His voice is unhurried, playful, then suddenly profound. The crowd erupts in laughter, then falls into the deepest collective silence you have ever been part of. Something lands in you — you'll feel it for days.

After

The satsang ends. People rise slowly — no one is in a hurry. Someone near you is weeping and smiling at the same time. You understand. You take the headset off. The room is the same. You are not.
